10 Interesting Facts About Managed IT Services You Should Know

Businesses in today's fast-paced, technology-driven world are continuously looking for methods to remain ahead of the curve and adapt to an ever-changing digital landscape. The proper administration of IT infrastructure and services is a critical component of this change, which has led to the growth of managed IT services as a dependable and cost-effective option. Managed IT services give firms access to competent IT project managed services specialists and cutting-edge technology, while also freeing up important time and resources that can be used elsewhere. In this blog post, we will look at some fascinating statistics about managed IT services as well as debunk some prevalent fallacies about them, throwing light on how these services may greatly help your firm.


Before we dive into the fascinating world of managed IT services, it's essential to understand the basics. Managed IT services involve outsourcing your organization's IT needs to a third-party provider, a managed services provider (MSP), offering expertise in a wide range of IT functions. These providers typically handle everything from network management and cybersecurity to data backup and disaster recovery, ensuring your business remains secure, compliant, and technologically up-to-date. So, without further ado, let's delve into the top 10 interesting managed IT service facts and clear up any misconceptions holding your business back from harnessing its full potential.

1. Increased Demand for Managed IT Services

Demand for managed IT services has been steadily increasing, owing to organizations' increasing reliance on technology and their need to keep up with the rapid pace of digital transformation. According to MarketsandMarkets, the global managed services market is expected to increase at an 8.1% compound annual growth rate (CAGR) from USD 223.0 billion in 2020 to USD 329.1 billion by 2025 (source: MarketsandMarkets). This expansion can be ascribed to a variety of causes, including increased efficiency, cost savings, and access to specialized expertise provided by managed IT services providers. As a result, companies in a variety of industries are turning to managed IT services as a strategic move to stay competitive and streamline their IT operations.


Myth: Outsourcing IT Means Giving Up Control


One prevalent misconception about managed IT services is that outsourcing your IT needs to a third-party supplier means you will lose control of your infrastructure and crucial systems. However, this is not the case. When you work with a managed IT services provider, you gain greater visibility and control over your IT infrastructure. Managed IT service providers collaborate closely with your organization to understand your specific demands and objectives, ensuring that their services are suited to your specific needs. Furthermore, they provide regular information and updates on the performance and security of your systems, allowing you to make informed IT infrastructure decisions. Managed IT services allow you to concentrate on your core business operations while a team of experts handles your IT demands successfully. and

2. A Broad Selection of Managed IT Service Providers

The managed IT services industry is made up of a large number of providers, each with differing levels of experience and a vast range of services tailored to specific company needs. According to a Spiceworks report, 61% of firms outsource at least one IT function to managed service providers. Because of the market's diversity, businesses can select a provider that best meets their specific objectives, goals, and budget. To establish a successful collaboration that propels your organization forward, carefully analyze potential providers' experience, service offerings, and track record.


Myth: Managed IT Services Are Expensive


One common misconception concerning managed IT services for small organizations is that they are prohibitively expensive. This belief, however, is unfounded. In actuality, managed IT services may save money for businesses of all sizes. You can reduce the need for a big in-house IT team by outsourcing your IT needs to a managed service provider, which can be both costly and time-consuming to manage. Furthermore, managed IT service providers provide scalable solutions that may be tailored to your company's development and changing needs. This flexibility allows you to pay just for the services you use, avoiding excessive costs and allowing you to manage resources more wisely. Finally, the cost savings and greater efficiency given by managed IT services may outweigh the initial expenditure, making it a worthwhile investment.

3. IT Management Services Save both time and money.

IT needs can be outsourced to managed service providers for considerable cost savings and greater business efficiency. According to a survey conducted by Computer Economics, organizations who outsource their IT services save an average of 25% on IT costs, with some estimating savings of up to 40%. It is because managed IT services providers provide less demand for in-house workers, streamlined operations, and access to specialist expertise. Furthermore, firms can use the time and money saved by outsourcing to focus on key capabilities and promote growth.


Myth: Managed IT Services Aren't Flexible


A typical misconception regarding managed IT services is that they require more flexibility, forcing organizations to comply to strict service options that may not meet their specific requirements. This, however, has to be more precise. Managed IT service providers recognize that each firm has unique demands, and they offer personalized solutions to meet those needs. Managed IT service providers can build and implement a personalized IT strategy that fits your organization's unique issues and objectives by collaborating closely with your business. This adaptability helps businesses to gain the full benefits of managed IT services while maintaining control over their IT environment and meeting their unique requirements.

4. Improved Security with Managed IT Services

Managed IT service providers play an important role in assisting organizations in protecting their data and infrastructure by implementing advanced security measures. According to an IBM report, the average data breach cost in 2022 will be $4.35 million, emphasizing the significance of strong cybersecurity measures. Businesses can gain access to a team of security specialists that are up to date on the newest cybersecurity risks and best practices by partnering with a managed IT services provider. These companies regularly monitor your systems, install up-to-date security policies, and guarantee that your firm complies with relevant rules, decreasing the risk of data breaches and other cyber threats dramatically.


Myth: Managed IT service providers cannot provide the same level of security as in-house IT teams.


Some argue that managed IT service companies can give a higher level of security than in-house IT employees. However, this is not the case. Managed IT services companies are experts in delivering IT solutions, including security, and have the skills, resources, and technology to protect your systems and data. They frequently have access to advanced security tools and threat intelligence that in-house teams may not have. Furthermore, managed IT services providers are dedicated to staying current with the ever-changing cybersecurity landscape, ensuring that your firm is secured against emerging threats. When compared to depending entirely on an in-house IT team, partnering with a managed IT services company can often improve security.


5. Service Offerings That Can Be Customized


One of the most important benefits of managed IT services is their adaptability, which enables organizations to customise the services they receive to their individual needs and requirements. According to a Techaisle report, 70% of small firms chose managed services because they provide configurable IT solutions and predictable pricing. Managed IT service providers collaborate closely with businesses to understand their specific IT difficulties and goals, producing tailored solutions that efficiently solve these concerns. This level of personalization allows businesses to optimize their IT infrastructure and services, increasing efficiency and fostering growth.


Myth: Managed IT Service Providers Provide One-Size-Fits-All Solutions.


One prevalent misperception regarding managed IT services is that providers give one-size-fits-all solutions that may not meet the unique demands of each firm. In reality, managed IT service providers understand that each firm is unique, and they seek to create personalized solutions that match each client's specific needs. Managed IT service providers can design tailored service options that generate the most value for your organization by engaging with your business to understand its goals, issues, and IT environment. This strategy ensures that you obtain the services and support that are best suited to your company, rather than being compelled to choose a pre-packaged solution that may only partially meet your needs.

6. Scalability is facilitated by managed IT services

Managed IT services are critical in assisting businesses in rapidly scaling their IT infrastructure as they grow. According to a Spiceworks poll, 42% of firms believe scalability to be one of the top reasons for employing managed IT services. Businesses can swiftly and easily alter their IT resources to accommodate expansion or changing needs by working with an operated IT services provider, without incurring large expenditures or operational issues. This versatility guarantees that your organization's IT infrastructure stays fluid and responsive, allowing you to grow and adapt in an ever-changing market.


Myth: Managed IT Services Restriction Business Growth


Some argue that managed IT services might stifle business growth by limiting IT resources and infrastructure. This, however, needs to be clarified. Managed IT service providers, on the other hand, are designed to assist and facilitate corporate growth by providing scalable and adaptive solutions. These service providers may assist businesses in expanding their IT infrastructure, implementing new technologies, and meeting changing demands as they grow. Managed IT service providers enable organizations to focus on their core capabilities and strategic ambitions rather than getting mired down in managing IT difficulties by providing the necessary resources and experience. It enables enterprises to expand more efficiently and effectively by using the power of managed IT services.

7. Increased availability and business continuity

One of the most significant benefits of managed IT services is their improved uptime and business continuity. According to a study by ITIC, Four in 10 enterprise organizations – 40% – indicate that a single hour of downtime can now cost their firms from $1 million to over $5 million – exclusive of legal fees, fines, or penalties. Managed IT service providers implement proactive monitoring and maintenance, identifying and addressing potential issues before they escalate into more significant problems that could cause downtime. Managed IT services help ensure your business operations run smoothly and efficiently by minimizing disruptions to your IT infrastructure.


Myth: Managed IT Services Cannot Ensure Uptime


Some people feel that managed IT services cannot ensure uptime or completely eliminate downtime. While no provider can guarantee 100% uptime, managed IT service providers are dedicated to minimizing downtime and ensuring company continuity. To discover and handle possible issues before they become critical, these providers use advanced monitoring tools and proactive maintenance practices. Furthermore, many managed IT services companies offer service level agreements (SLAs) that explain their commitment to uptime and the compensation you'll receive in the event of a violation. You can greatly enhance your IT infrastructure's uptime and reduce the chance of costly downtime by working with a managed IT services provider, ensuring that your business remains active and productive.

8. Vendor-Independent Managed IT Service Providers

Businesses benefit greatly from vendor-neutral managed IT service providers because they provide unbiased recommendations and assistance for a wide range of technologies and solutions. According to a Valtix report, 95% of businesses are moving toward a multi-cloud environment, emphasizing the need of having a managed IT services provider that can efficiently manage and support several platforms and vendors. Businesses can ensure that they receive the finest technology solutions for their specific needs by working with a vendor-neutral provider rather than being limited to the offerings of a single vendor.


Myth: Vendor-neutral managed IT service providers are inexperienced.


Some argue that vendor-neutral managed IT service providers require additional skills to support a wide range of technologies and solutions. However, this is not the case. Vendor-neutral providers frequently have substantial expertise and experience across a wide range of platforms and suppliers, allowing them to provide complete assistance and counsel for your organization's IT requirements. By remaining vendor-agnostic, these providers may objectively assess and offer the best solutions for your company without being swayed by vendor-specific biases or constraints. It ensures that your firm receives the best IT assistance and solutions that are targeted to your specific needs and goals. ‍

9. Managed IT Services' Role in Disaster Recovery ‍

Managed IT services are essential in assisting firms in developing and implementing solid disaster recovery plans. According to a Datto survey, 91% of managed service providers revealed that their clients were victims of ransomware attacks in 2021, highlighting the need of having a comprehensive disaster recovery strategy in place. Businesses can ensure that their data is frequently backed up and that their systems can be rapidly restored in the event of a disaster or cyberattack by working with a managed IT services provider. This preventive approach reduces downtime and potential data loss, thereby preserving your company's reputation and bottom line. ‍


Myth: Disaster Recovery Is Only for Large Companies


One prevalent misperception about disaster recovery is that it is only required for large businesses. Disasters, hacks, and system failures, on the other hand, can damage enterprises of all sizes. Due to limited resources and IT competence, small and medium-sized businesses may be considerably more exposed to these catastrophes. Managed IT service providers may assist firms in developing and implementing disaster recovery plans that are suited to their individual requirements, ensuring that they are prepared for potential disruptions and can quickly recover their systems and data. Businesses of all kinds, regardless of size or industry, may protect their operations and assets by investing in disaster recovery.

10. Compliance with Managed IT Services

Any firm can find it difficult to navigate the complicated environment of industry rules and compliance standards. Managed IT service providers may assist firms in staying in compliance with current standards and regulations, lowering the risk of fines and reputational harm. A Fortra analysis found that the cost of compliance can range from $5.5 million to nearly $22 million, emphasizing the significance of following data protection standards. Businesses may guarantee that their IT infrastructure and operations are compliant by employing the expertise and resources of a managed IT services provider, safeguarding them from potential penalties and legal difficulties.



Myth: Managed IT Service Providers Are Unable to Keep Up With Changing Compliance Requirements


Some argue that managed IT service providers require assistance in order to stay up with the ever-changing world of compliance standards. This, however, needs to be clarified. Managed IT service providers provide the experience and resources to stay current on regulatory developments, ensuring that your organization's IT infrastructure remains compliant. These service providers collaborate with businesses to understand their specific compliance requirements, monitor applicable rules, and execute necessary updates or adjustments to ensure compliance. Businesses can offload the burden of compliance management by partnering with a managed IT services provider, allowing them to focus on their core activities while being compliant with industry rules.


Final Thoughts


Finally, managed IT services have numerous advantages that can considerably increase the productivity, security, and scalability of your organization's IT infrastructure. Businesses can have access to diverse expertise, customised solutions, and guarantee that their IT infrastructure is well-maintained and compliance with relevant legislation by working with a managed IT services provider. Leveraging the potential of managed IT services is a strategic move that can position your firm for long-term success as technology advances and the business landscape becomes more competitive. Don't let common misconceptions keep you from investigating the benefits of managed IT services; the potential benefits greatly exceed the misconceptions.
